AI WORKFLOW #1

AP Invoice Automation

Vendor invoice in. Zoho bill out.

The Pain

6-10 minutes per invoice. 30-80 invoices a month at the sweet spot. That's 2-4 hours every week your bookkeeper spends typing numbers that already exist on a PDF.

The Build

AI reads the invoice PDF → extracts vendor, invoice #, dates, line items, totals → creates the bill in Zoho Books (or QuickBooks Online / Xero) → logs the record to an independent audit trail → emails your bookkeeper a link to review in the accounting system's native UI.

Your bookkeeper never stops being the reviewer. They just stop being the typist.

The Result

~7 hours per month returned to the person who runs your back office. Plus audit-ready records every month — the independent log survives migrations between accounting systems.

Direct-fit ICP: $5-20M revenue, 30-80 invoices/month, running Zoho / QBO / Xero.

AI WORKFLOW #2

Customer Setup from Service Agreement

Signed Service Agreement in. Zoho customer out.

The Pain

Sales closes a deal. The signed agreement sits in an inbox. Your bookkeeper eventually chases it down, reads it line-by-line, types it into Zoho — and hopes the billing contact is actually in the agreement (it often isn't). Days pass before the first invoice goes out. Cash flow suffers.

The Build

AI reads the signed Service Agreement → extracts legal name, DBA, billing address, payment terms, billing contact, tax status → creates the customer record in Zoho Books → flags any missing fields in the review email so your bookkeeper knows what to chase before the first invoice.

Works with or without a CRM. No HubSpot, Salesforce, or CRM migration required.

The Result

~1-2 hours per month returned plus faster speed-to-first-invoice — which means cash in the door days sooner. Clean customer master data from day one.

Lower-volume than AP, but high-leverage: a new-customer setup delay is a direct hit to cash flow.

AI WORKFLOW #3

Vendor Setup + W-9 Reconciliation

Vendor Agreement + W-9 in. Reconciled vendor record + clean 1099 data out.

The Pain

Two documents to reconcile. The legal name on the vendor agreement rarely matches the W-9 exactly. Your bookkeeper defers vendor setup to the first invoice, files the W-9 somewhere (or not at all), then spends every January chasing missing W-9s and reconciling names before filing 1099s.

The single most stressful week of the bookkeeper's year — avoidable entirely.

The Build

AI reads both the vendor agreement and the W-9 → extracts company info from each → reconciles (W-9 takes precedence on legal name and tax ID fields, since that's what the IRS sees) → auto-emails the vendor if the names mismatch and requests confirmation → TIN-validates, dedups, and creates the vendor record in Zoho with the W-9 as the source of truth.

The Result

~2-4 hours per month returned. Plus year-end 1099 filing becomes a review step — the 1099 list writes itself from the audit log your bookkeeper has been building all year.

The workflow that makes experienced bookkeepers lean forward. January 1099 week becomes a non-event.
